About NYC Sun Times

New York City experiences typical mid-latitude sunrise and sunset patterns. During late September, the city sees nearly equal day and night hours as we approach the autumn equinox. The sun rises in the east over the East River and sets in the west over the Hudson River and New Jersey.

Best Times for Photography

Golden Hour: The hour before sunset (5:49 PM - 6:49 PM) provides the warmest, most flattering light for photography. The city's skyscrapers create beautiful shadows and the light has a golden quality.

Blue Hour: 20-30 minutes after sunset when the sky turns deep blue but city lights are visible. Perfect for cityscape photography.

Seasonal Variations

NYC sun times vary significantly throughout the year. Summer solstice brings sunrise around 5:25 AM and sunset near 8:30 PM, while winter solstice sees sunrise around 7:20 AM and sunset at 4:30 PM.
